Understanding Your Mortgage

A simple breakdown of how your monthly payment is calculated.

Loan Principal

This is the amount you borrow after making your down payment. It's calculated as Property Price minus Down Payment. Your monthly payments go toward repaying this principal plus interest.

Interest Rate

The annual interest rate charged by the lender. A lower rate means lower monthly payments. We use the standard monthly compounding formula to compute your amortized payments.

Loan Term

The duration over which you repay the loan, typically 10–30 years. Longer terms reduce monthly payments but increase total interest paid over the life of the loan.

Down Payment

A larger down payment reduces your loan amount and may qualify you for better interest rates. We recommend at least 20% of the property price for optimal loan terms.

Monthly Breakdown

Each monthly payment consists of principal and interest portions. Early payments are interest-heavy; over time, more of your payment goes toward reducing the principal balance.
